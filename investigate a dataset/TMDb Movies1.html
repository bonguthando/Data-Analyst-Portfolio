#Defining a funtion to separate the genre column.
def film(column):
    film = data[column].str.cat(sep = '|') #this allows for the column to be separated by the character '|'.
    film = pd.Series(film.split('|')) #I now store the values separately using pandas series.
    film_order = film.value_counts(ascending = False) #Arrange in descending order.
    
    return film_order

Limitations¶

There may be inaccurate figures in the dataset which would lead to false representation of some findings. One example was found when comparing the top 20 movies by budget on this dataset against "List of highest-gross films" found on wikipedia, here there is a movie title "The Net" with a revenue of 1,106,279,658.00 USD but it is not present on the Wikipedia list, further more revenue on the Box Office Mojo shows that it is in fact 110,627,965.00 USD.

Other limitations are my inability to successfully run a stacked barplot without showing errors to visually display the relationship between revenue and profit of the top 20 movies.

Conclusions¶

There are many factors that contribute to a movie's success, but with this information one can see that more moviegoers go out to watch movies with higher budgets. They also do not mind spending between 120 and 140 minutes to watch these movies, with action and adventure having 14 and 15 movies in the top 20 (note: a single movie can be of multiple genres) proving to be the two most popular of all the genres. To show up in the top 20 movies had a minimum budget of 100,000,000.00USD while 'Minions' and 'The Lord of The Rings' were outliers that budgets of 95,000,000.00 or less.
